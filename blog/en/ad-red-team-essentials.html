üîë Kerberoasting: The Stealthy Attack

Reflection: Kerberoasting is an exploit where the attacker extracts password hashes from service accounts. A configuration flaw that can be fatal. #ADSecurity #Kerberos

Kerberoasting is a classic, silent, and extremely effective attack in Active Directory. It exploits the Kerberos protocol and the fact that Service Principal Names (SPNs)‚Äîused by service accounts‚Äîdo not require the client (the attacker) to prove knowledge of the password to request the service account's password hash.

The Risk

If a service account has a weak or never-changed password, the attacker can obtain the hash and crack the password offline, gaining high-privilege access.

Mitigation

Requires robust and long passwords for service accounts, and continuous monitoring to detect unusual Kerberos ticket (TGS) requests.
