Active Directory Red Team Specialist (AD-RTS): Building Offensive Expertise in 2025

CERTIFICATION REVIEW | 10 MIN READ
Overview: The AD-RTS certification focuses on offensive strategies in Windows environments, teaching skills like credential extraction and network pivoting. It prepares professionals to simulate persistent threats effectively.

Active Directory (AD) serves as the foundation for identity management in enterprise environments, with over 90% of Fortune 1000 companies utilizing it to manage endpoints and assets. For red teamers, mastering AD vulnerabilities is essential, as it underpins nearly all corporate networks globally.

The AD-RTS Course Structure

The AD-RTS course, offered by platforms like CyberWarfare Labs, is a hands-on program designed for red team enthusiasts, penetration testers, and offensive cyber analysts. It includes over 4 hours of HD videos, course PDFs, and two attack path write-ups.

The curriculum is divided into six critical sections:

  • AD Security: Covering core components like Schema, Global Catalog, NTDS.dit database, Group Policy, and abused roles.
  • Certificate Services Security: Authority templates, web enrollment, revocation lists, and common attacks (ADCS).
  • Abusing Exchange Server Roles: Outlook Web Access, Exchange Web Services, persistence via mailbox delegation.
  • Attack Surface of ESXi: Domain-joined scenarios and administrator role demos.
  • Cyber Range Labs: Simulating unauthenticated and authenticated adversaries.

Practical Skills & Methods

Participants gain practical skills in weaponizing misconfigurations, exploiting services, and executing full red-team operations, including initial access via DNS abuse and LDAP recon, privilege escalation through SQL Server compromise and credential dumping, lateral movement via certificate impersonation and RCE on Domain Controllers, and data exfiltration from ESXi hosts and guest VMs.

Prerequisites & Benefits

Prerequisites include basic web and network penetration testing knowledge, familiarity with command-line environments, and an attacker VM like Kali or Parrot. Benefits encompass lifetime access to materials, technical support via email and Discord, and a CWL Verified AD-RTS Certificate upon capturing all lab flags.

Real-world applications involve simulating attacks on enterprise AD, Exchange, and hypervisors, aligning with certifications like SANS SEC565, which overlaps in AD attacks, lateral movement, and Bloodhound analysis.
